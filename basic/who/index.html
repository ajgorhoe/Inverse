Who Develops INVERSE


Principal Developer:

    Igor Gresovnik created Invese in spring 1994. He is now running further development of the software and bringing into reality many new ideas in all areas of development.

Developers:

    Damjan Jelovsek made his university final degree in summer 1998 using Inverse. After that he joined the development of Inverse. His main area of development are interfaces with simulation programmes and tools for shape optimization.

    Damijan Markovic and Janez Povh joined the Inverse development team in autumn 1998. Their area of development are optimization algorithms, supporting utilities and interfaces.

Backup Team:

    The C3M's research team lead by Tomaz Rodic represent a backup team for Inverse development. New features of the software are first tested by this group. The main research area of the group are numerical simulations in continuum mechanics. Many new ideas in Inverse development are therefore encouraged by the needs related to optimization and inverse problems in this field.

    The development of a finite element driver which began in C3M in 1998 is closely related to the development of Inverse. The development is carried out by Tomaz Sustar in cooperation with Joze Korelc and his SMS. Some functions that were developed for Inverse will be used in this driver and vice versa, some results of the driver development will be used in the development of Inverse.
